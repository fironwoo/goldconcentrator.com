## Project positioning

This is a small to medium alluvial gold reference case. The useful lesson is not a universal 20 TPH package, but the way a short recovery section can be added when the client already has road-construction equipment, site preparation and some front-end handling capacity.

The project task was to separate gold from the 0-4 mm material stream and deliver a practical gravity circuit around the available feed.

## Known project facts

- Mineral: alluvial gold
- Location: Romania
- Nominal capacity: 20 TPH
- Feed description: sand larger than 4 mm reported by the supplied notes
- Target product size: 0-2 mm fraction for recovery
- Route: washing, screening and gravity separation

## Process route

The route stays deliberately short:

1. Screen and control the feed fraction.
2. Recover suitable gold-bearing material with centrifugal concentration.
3. Use sluice and shaking table duty for concentrate handling and upgrading.
4. Include water pumping and small supporting items in the scope, not only the main concentrator.

## Equipment scope

The supplied notes listed a vibrating screen, STLB80 centrifugal concentrator, 1 x 6 m sluice, 2100 shaking table and IS80 water pump. For quotation work, the important point is that the pump, table, sluice, screening, pipework and spares belong in the same review as the main recovery machine.

## Quotation and design lessons

A small alluvial project can look simple, but underquoting often happens when the offer only lists the visible concentrator. The actual plant duty also needs water, screening, concentrate cleaning, hose or pipe connections, spare parts and site interface checks.

This reference is useful for early conversations about compact 20 TPH alluvial gold routes, especially where the client already controls excavation, access and basic feed preparation.
