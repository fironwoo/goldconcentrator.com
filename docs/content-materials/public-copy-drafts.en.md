Body:

## Project challenge

The feed contained clay-bound gravel and mixed particle sizes. A simple screen and sluice route would not be enough unless the valuable particles were released from clay before separation.

## Information required before design

The review should confirm clay behavior, top feed size, water availability, gold particle size and whether fine liberated gold remains in the screened fraction.

## Process direction

The route should begin with controlled feeding and washing, followed by size classification. Coarse and medium fractions can then be routed to suitable gravity stages, while fine fractions should only receive additional recovery equipment when testing shows recoverable gold remains.

## Project challenge

The customer needed a route for primary or weathered gold ore. The key question was not which concentrator to buy first, but where the gold was locked and what grind size was justified.

## Information required before design

Representative ore samples, assay data, mineral observations, target throughput, hardness information and test response should be checked before equipment sizing.

## Project challenge

The project required a route that could improve chromite concentrate quality while controlling slimes and unstable feed size.

## Information required before design

The review should confirm feed grade, size distribution, liberation, slime content, water condition and the required Cr2O3 or buyer specification.

## Short Video Scripts

### Video 1: Alluvial Gold Sample Review

Hook:
Before choosing a gold wash plant, check what the ore prevents the gold from doing.

This sample review looks at clay behavior, feed size, visible gold size and water conditions. If the gold is already liberated but locked in clay, washing and disaggregation become the first design problem. CTA:

### Video 2: Hard Rock Gold Flowsheet Logic

Hook:
A crusher, mill and concentrator package is not automatically a gold flowsheet.

Script:
Hard rock gold processing starts with liberation. The route should define how fine the ore needs to be ground, where gravity recovery belongs and whether gravity is enough for the mineral association. Grinding finer than necessary increases power, wear and slime generation.

CTA:
Share your ore type, target TPH and any assay or test data before selecting equipment.

### Video 3: Gravity Concentration Equipment Roles

Hook:
Gravity equipment works best when each machine receives the right size range.

Script:
Jigs, spirals, centrifugal concentrators and shaking tables do different jobs. A good flowsheet classifies the feed, sends each fraction to the right recovery stage and cleans concentrate only where it adds value.

CTA:
Start with the ore and process route, then select equipment around the flowsheet.
